# Market research — honest, no false precision

## Bottom-up sizing only

Count specific reachable customers × realistic price. That's your size band.

- Reject top-down "1% of a $10B market = $100M" reasoning. It's fiction.
- TAM / SAM / SOM is fine as **order-of-magnitude framing**, not as
  fake-precise numbers. A band ("hundreds to low thousands of customers at
  $50–100/mo") beats a spurious "$4.2M TAM."
- If you can't name where the first 10 customers come from, you don't have a
  market estimate, you have a wish.

## The real incumbent is "do nothing"

Most of the time you're not competing with another product — you're competing
with a spreadsheet, a paper form, a group chat, or ignoring the problem. The
exploitable gap is the **friction in the current workaround**. Map it precisely.

## Signal vs noise

- **Signal:** a detailed description of a manual workaround someone actually
  runs. Specifics, steps, time spent, money spent.
- **Noise:** generalized venting. "This whole industry is broken" tells you
  nothing actionable.
- A complaint with hundreds of upvotes is a **documented pattern**, not an
  anecdote — but upvotes are still `said`, not `paid`.

## Anti-vanity

Interest is not demand.

| Looks like traction | Actually is |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Signups, waitlist | said (interest) |
| Page views, clicks | said (interest) |
| Upvotes, likes | said (interest) |
| Built a workaround | did (commitment) |
| Deposit / pre-order / LOI | paid (demand) |

The forge classifies every vanity metric as weak `said`, never `paid`. If your
"traction" is all in the top three rows, you have interest, not a business.

## Reachability test

You must name **2+ places you can reach the segment this week**. If you can't,
either the segment is wrong or you don't understand it yet. Reachability is part
of the market, not an afterthought.
